Why should you keep your furnace serviced?

As much as we may not like to think about it, our furnaces are one of our homes’ most essential equipment. They keep us warm and comfortable all winter long, so it’s necessary to ensure they’re in good shape.

One of the best ways to keep your furnace running smoothly is to have it serviced regularly. This involves a professional inspection and cleaning of the unit, which can help prevent problems down the road.

Here are just a few of the many benefits of furnace service.

Keep Your Home Warm and Comfortable

During the winter, your furnace works hard to keep your home warm and comfortable. You can help by ensuring that your home is well-insulated and taking steps to reduce drafts.

One way to keep your furnace from working overtime is to ensure your home is well-insulated. Checking for cracks or gaps in your walls and ceilings can help you identify areas where heat might be escaping.

Caulking and weatherstripping can seal these openings and help keep the heat inside your home.

Another way to reduce drafts is to look at your doors and windows. Drafty doors and windows can let cold air into your home, making it harder for your furnace to do its job. Replacing worn weatherstripping and adding door sweeps can help eliminate drafts.

Ensure Safe Operation

Your furnace is complex equipment that must operate safely to prevent accidents. During furnace service, your technician will check all the safety features to ensure they’re working correctly. It Will gives you peace of mind knowing that your furnace is safe to use all winter.

The pressure switch is one of your furnace’s most critical safety features. The button is designed to shut off the gas supply to the burner if the pressure in the combustion

chamber gets too high. If this switch is faulty, it could allow gas to keep flowing to the burner even when the pressure is too high, which could cause an explosion. That’s why it’s essential to have a professional check this switch during furnace service.

Another safety feature that will check during furnace service is the flame sensor. This sensor is designed to detect whether or not a flame is present in the combustion chamber. Who will shut off the gas supply if no flame is current? It prevents dangerous situations where gas could build up and explode.
